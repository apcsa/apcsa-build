BlueJ, the Programming Environment

Programming is best learned by doing it, not simply watching or hearing someone talk about it.

You'll spend most of your time in this course actually programming. In the first few weeks you'll get up to speed learning new vocabulary and concepts. Towards the end you'll spend most of your time writing real programs that do sophisticated things.

Professional programmers use special systems called development environments to speed their coding. In this course, you'll use the BlueJ development environment, a simple yet relatively powerful system written especially for introductory programming courses.

The BlueJ system isn't contained in the UCCP Course window -- it runs as a separate application on your computer. As you work through this course you will switch regularly between the UCCP and BlueJ windows.

Note: BlueJ must be installed in order to run this course. We'll take a closer look at the BlueJ System in the next lesson.
