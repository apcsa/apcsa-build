Basically, we have a pattern that combines behavior with information:

"To make a ________ milk shake, combine ________ with vanilla ice cream and milk in a blender."

The information is what flavor milkshake is desired and what fruit or flavor of ice cream to use.

In the alarm clock example, we represented the similarity information as state variables associated with the object. An alternative is to associate the information only with a particular behavior—in this case, milk shake making. A piece of information that's used in this relatively minor way is called a parameter.
